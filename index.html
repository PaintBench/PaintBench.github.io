PaintBench

Tasks trivial in any raster editor, still out of reach for frontier AI.

A dynamically scalable benchmark spanning 20 primitive visual editing operations. Procedurally generated, deterministically evaluated with pure pixel math — no model judges.

The highest-performing model we evaluated (Nano Banana 2) scores only 23.5%.

arXiv Paper GitHub Dataset Results

Primitive Operations

20 atomic task types: geometric transformation, structural manipulation, color change, and symbolic reasoning.

Deterministically Evaluated

Every problem has exactly one correct output. Pure pixel math — like HumanEval for code, but for images.

Dynamically Scalable

Generated from random seeds — fresh, unseen problems on demand. Benchmark saturation is impossible by design.

Model Performance

mIoU (%): mean Intersection-over-Union integrated over 11 CIE ΔE₇₆ thresholds. Bars shown out of 100% to highlight remaining headroom. Click a row to expand per-category scores.

TinyGrafixBench

Can failures on geometric primitives predict performance on real-world charts? We applied PaintBench's framework to data visualization editing.

600 problems across 5 chart types and 4 editing operations each, procedurally generated and deterministically evaluated.

Model rankings strongly correlate between PaintBench and TinyGrafixBench, validating our primitive benchmark as a proxy for harder real-world tasks.
